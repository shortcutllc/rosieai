5. Design Principles

The rules that guide every layout and interaction decision

MOAT

Smart Prompt > Action Cards

Replace 3 generic action cards with 1 contextual suggestion. Rosie knows the last feed was left side 2h ago, so she suggests "Right Side" next. One tap, not three decisions. The prompt gets smarter with more data.

UX

Compact Stats > Progress Rings

Stats rings are visually heavy and duplicate the action cards. Replace with compact stat chips that show the number + "of ~X typical" context. Parents need "am I on track?" not a dashboard.

AI

Proactive Alerts

Only appear when the AI has something worth saying. Growth spurt detected, developmental leap approaching, unusual pattern noticed. Not a daily notification — a contextual insight that earns its screen space.

UX

Daily Plan = Actionable Value

Parents ask "what should I do with my baby today?" The Daily Plan answers with 3 age-appropriate activities tied to current developmental focus. Checkable for satisfaction. This is the stickiest content on the Home tab.

MOAT

Conversational Logging

"Just text Rosie" to log events. "Fed right side 15 min" becomes a structured event. The chat input serves dual purpose: questions AND logging. The placeholder says "Log or ask anything..." — this is the anti-tracking tracker.

MOAT

Discover = Location + Development

"This Week" becomes "Discover" — merging developmental content with hyperlocal age-appropriate activities and weather-aware suggestions. "There's a music class at the YMCA, 10 min walk." No other baby app does this.

UX

Milestone Skill Tree > Checklist

Checklists create anxiety ("my baby hasn't checked this box"). Skill trees show progression: Done → Emerging → Next. Parents see what their baby IS doing, not what they haven't done yet. Celebrates progress, not gaps.

EMOTIONAL

Night Mode = Empathy Mode

10pm-6am: dark indigo, minimal content, no stats, no carousel. Just the smart prompt, quick log, and a reassurance message. The 3am parent needs "you're doing great" not a dashboard. Chat responses are shorter and warmer.

AI

Voice-First for One-Handed Use

The voice button is always visible. Hold to speak, release to send. "Just fed, right side, she was fussy." Parsed into structured event + conversational response. Critical for feeding with one hand.

6b. Onboarding Voice & Copy Principles

The tone rules behind every screen

VOICE

Questions, Not Forms

"What should I call you?" not "Enter your name." "When did Lily make her grand entrance?" not "Date of birth." Every field is a conversation, not a form. The parent is talking to Rosie, not filling out a government document.

DELIGHT

Buttons Have Personality

"That's me" → "Love that name" → "Almost done" → "Let's go!" Each button is a micro-moment of warmth. No "Next" "Next" "Next" "Submit." The progression builds momentum and makes the parent smile.

SMART

Show Value Immediately

"That makes Lily 6 months old ✨" appears the instant they enter a date. The celebration screen shows a personalized Rosie quote. Before the parent has logged a single event, they can see the AI already knows something about their baby.

PACE

One Thing Per Screen

Each screen asks exactly one question. No multi-field forms. No scrolling. The progress dots show 4 steps — but the whole flow takes under 60 seconds. Speed builds trust: "This app respects my time."

TONE

Playful, Not Silly

"Star of the show" and "grand entrance" are warm and fun without being cheesy. The humor is gentle — a parent at 3am with a newborn should still feel welcomed, not annoyed. If a joke wouldn't land in the NICU, cut it.

TRUST

No Pressure, Ever

"No pressure either way" on the prematurity question. "Nickname totally counts." Skip is always visible. Rosie never guilts a parent into sharing more. This builds the trust foundation that the entire AI relationship depends on.
